The History of Lavender

Lavender, native to the Mediterranean, has been used for centuries in perfumes, medicine, and bathing rituals, with its name derived from the Latin lavare, meaning "to wash." The color lavender, inspired by the flower's soft purple hue, entered the English language in the 18th century and became associated with elegance and calm. Both the plant and color symbolize healing, tranquility, and subtle individuality.
